Guided by the theme "Thriving Through Turbulence: Resilience, Innovation & Collaboration in Global Trade", the Congress convened over 700 regional registrants representing 79 industries, reflecting a broad and diverse cross-section of the global economy. Across the two-day programme, 75 speakers contributed their expertise through 25 sessions, offering delegates rich and multifaceted insights into the forces shaping global commerce.

The Congress underscored the importance of fostering dialogue that brings together diverse perspectives to address the complexities of today's trade environment. Delegates recognised that while policies, treaties, and legal frameworks provide the scaffolding of trade, it is the trust between people and the strength of relationships that ensure commerce continues across borders.

A particular focus was placed on the evolving responsibilities of in-house counsel in navigating uncertainty. No longer confined to a purely interpretive role, legal professionals are increasingly called upon to anticipate regulatory change, guide strategic decision-making, and enable sustainable growth.

Beyond knowledge-sharing, the Congress was a forum for translating ideas into practical strategies. Delegates were encouraged to carry forward insights that can inform decision-making, strengthen institutional resilience, and enhance collaboration across borders.
